# PLAN

## Progression

1) Hatch from egg into a larvae. As a larvae, only have minimal gameplay (only drag mechanics, small obstacles, no enemies)
2) As you eat more, you increase the gameplay with more difficulty (Add hazards, stuff that slows you down). At one point we get to introduce you to sling-shotting yourself and have a few places it's necessary to use it.
3) Once you get to the top of the tree, there's a cutscene where you curl up into a cocoon and then turn into a butterfly, flying away. EOG (END OF GAME).

## Design

### Controls:
Mouse controls

### Moves

- Climbing
  - \*Primarily design all levels to facilitate this mechanic
  - To move either the front or the rear end of the caterpillar - mouse down/click to detach that end of the caterpillar from the tree, keeping the mouse held, that end of the caterpillar will follow the cursor to reposition, then when the player lets go of the mouse, that end of the caterpillar will attach to the tree at its current location.
  - Depletes some stamina while repositioning
- Slingshotting
  - Clicking and dragging the center node to the caterpillar’s left or right
  - Depletes a large amount of stamina

### Mechanics

- Stamina
  - Replenishes when all parts are clinging to the tree
  - Forces player to make decisions faster the lower on stamina the caterpillar is.

### Obstacles
- Sap surface
  - Slows you down and depletes stamina
- Wet/slippery surface
  - If the player lets go of the mouse, that end of the caterpillar overlapping it will just fall down and dangle
- Crevaces
  - A gap in the side of the tree you'll need to traverse, primarily by sling-shotting.
  - Falling into it makes you restart at the beginning of a section
